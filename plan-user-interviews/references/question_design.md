# Question Design Guidelines

## Principles of Good Interview Questions

1.  **Ask for specifics, not generalizations.**
    *   **Poor:** "Do you usually use our app for booking?"
    *   **Better:** "Tell me about the last time you booked a flight."
2.  **Avoid leading questions.**
    *   **Poor:** "How frustrated were you when the system crashed?"
    *   **Better:** "How did you feel when the system crashed?" or "What was your reaction when that happened?"
3.  **Focus on past behavior, not future predictions.**
    *   **Poor:** "Would you pay $5/month for this feature?"
    *   **Better:** "How do you currently solve this problem, and how much time/money do you spend on it?"
4.  **Use open-ended questions.**
    *   **Poor:** "Is the new dashboard easy to use?"
    *   **Better:** "Can you describe your experience using the new dashboard?"
5.  **Embrace silence.**
    *   Instruct the PM to wait a few seconds after the user answers to see if they offer more detail organically.

## Common Probing Prompts

*   "Tell me more about that."
*   "Can you give me an example?"
*   "Why is that important to you?"
*   "What did you do next?"
*   "How did that make you feel?"
*   "What were you expecting to happen?"
